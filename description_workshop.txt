[h1]Pet Bestiary[/h1]

Unlock, manage, dye, and display multiple pets from a Bestiary-style UI.

Pet Bestiary lets you equip multiple normal pets and light pets at once without relying on Terraria’s normal single pet slot. Once a pet item enters your inventory, that pet is permanently unlocked for your character. From there, you can activate pets, save presets, assign dyes, randomize pets, and build your collection through the Pet Bestiary.

The default hotkey for opening and closing the Pet Bestiary is [b]P[/b]. You can also open it with the button next to the trash icon.

[h2]Features[/h2]

- Equip multiple normal pets and light pets at once
- Pet limits increased up to 9999 per category
- Custom Bestiary-inspired pet UI
- Separate Normal Pet and Light Pet sections
- Unlock pets by obtaining their summon item
- Vanilla pet unlock hints
- Modded pet detection where possible
- Search bar for finding pets quickly
- Filter pets by origin, such as Vanilla, Calamity Mod, and other detected mods
- Collection progress bar at the bottom of the Bestiary
- Hovering over the progress bar shows whether Progression Mode is enabled
- Pet presets
- Equip All and Unequip All buttons
- Lock pets to protect them from bulk actions
- Optional progression mode for unlocking more pet slots over time
- Visual pet spacing with a spacing preference slider
- Random pet button that equips a random unlocked pet that is not currently equipped
- Manual close button in the top-right corner
- General UI cleanup and polish
- Debug tools for testing and troubleshooting

[h2]Prismatic Palette[/h2]

Pet Bestiary now includes the [b]Prismatic Palette[/b], a dedicated dye selector for pets.

Dyeing pets no longer requires owning multiple copies of a dye. Once you collect a dye, it is unlocked in the Prismatic Palette. Clicking the dye option on a pet opens the Prismatic Palette, where you can reuse any unlocked dye as much as you want, on as many pets as you want.

Prismatic Palette features:

- Unlock dyes by collecting them
- Assign dyes to individual pets
- Search bar for finding dyes quickly
- Random dye button
- Dye All button for currently equipped pets
- Clear All Dyes button for currently equipped pets
- Dye items are not consumed, moved, stored, or deleted

[h2]Mod Compatibility[/h2]

Pet Bestiary attempts to detect pets from other mods automatically. If a modded pet can be identified, it should appear in the Bestiary.

Some modded pets may show ??? for unlock conditions if the source cannot be determined automatically. Some custom-drawn modded pets may also have limited dye or preview support.

[h2]Known Bugs / Limitations[/h2]

- Some custom-drawn modded pets can disable dyes for the rest of your pets.
- Currently known pet with this behavior: Thief’s Dime from Calamity Mod.
- Some pets cannot be displayed fully or at all in the preview, such as Exo Gemstone and Miniature Elemental Heart from Calamity’s Vanities.
- Some modded pet unlock conditions may be unknown and display as ???.
- Modded pet support depends on how each mod implements its pets.

[h2]Safety[/h2]

Pet Bestiary does not use real extra pet equipment slots and does not store your pet or dye items. Everything is handled through a virtual pet system to avoid item loss.

This was a major design goal. When I was originally working on More Pet Slots and experimenting with individual dye slots, the custom dye slot could sometimes eat dyes. I do not want anyone losing items, so Pet Bestiary avoids storing real pet or dye items entirely.
